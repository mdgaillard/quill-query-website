Meet Munin.

Guardian of the Query

Dragons throughout many myths have been known to guard hoards of treasures. And Munin has an affinity for a particular kind. His vault is one filled with decades of carefully preserved clinical evidence.

  • Every coin in their hoard represents a recorded data point.
  • Every scroll represents a published manuscript.
  • Every gem reflects real-world evidence translated into discovery and better patient care.

Research is one of the few treasures that grows more valuable with time. A well-kept registry can answer questions decades after the first patient was enrolled, revealing patterns no one knew to look for when the data were first collected.

That is the treasure Munin protects.

Meet Quill.

Keeper of the Quill

Ravens have carried stories across kingdoms for centuries. Quillon represents the ferrying of discoveries from the quiet shelves of the archive to the places where they can make a difference: peer-reviewed journals, conference halls, classrooms, boardrooms, and conversations between colleagues.

  • Every paper begins with a question.
  • Every presentation begins with a story.
  • Every carefully chosen word is another chance to make complex evidence accessible.

Quill carries communication from years of careful data stewardship into the wider world, inspiring evidence-based decisions that help clinicians, researchers, and patients improve care.

Quillon never stays in the library for long.

Yes, the dragon is named after a raven, and the raven is named after a dragon-like creature. Everyone is perfectly happy with this arrangement.
