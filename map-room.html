The Map Room

A Career, Charted

Longitudinal research rewards the long view, so here is one; the years plotted like coordinates, from the first record to the archive as it stands today.

[ placeholder milestones - dates and details to be refined ]

  1. 2006

    The First Record

    Clinical Research Coordinator

    Joined a high-volume orthopedic surgical practice and entered the first cases of what would become a long-term outcomes program, learning early that a follow-up letter mailed on time is a form of scholarship.

  2. 2009

    The Registry Takes Shape

    Registry Architect

    Designed and built the longitudinal database itself - schema, data dictionary, and follow-up workflows - replacing scattered spreadsheets with a single system built to outlast any one study.

  3. 2012

    First Words in Print

    Medical Writer

    The registry produced its first peer-reviewed publications, and the writing half of the practice began in earnest - manuscripts, abstracts, and conference presentations drawn from the growing cohort.

  4. 2015

    Under Regulation

    Study Manager, FDA-Regulated Research

    Took on IDE- and PMA-governed studies, where every data point carries regulatory weight. Protocol adherence, monitoring visits, and submission-grade documentation joined the daily craft.

  5. 2019

    The Long Middle

    Senior Research & Data Lead

    The cohort crossed ten-year follow-up, the point where a registry stops describing a procedure and starts describing a lifetime. Survivorship analyses, metal ion surveillance, and risk stratification work deepened accordingly.

  6. 2022

    Director of the Archive

    Clinical Research Director

    Assumed direction of the research program end to end - study design, data systems, analysis, writing, and the mentoring of the next set of hands to keep the records faithfully.

  7. Today

    The Archive as It Stands

    Quill & Query, Concurrently

    More than 7,700 cases, follow-up reaching nineteen years, and seventeen-plus publications, with the next questions already waiting in the data.

The Quill & the Query Preserving data. Producing discovery.
