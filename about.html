The Library

About the Scholar

Every long-term registry is a conversation with time.

One patient becomes one hundred, then thousands. Years become decades. Trends begin to emerge naturally from this carefully preserved evidence. That is the work I have spent my career building.

For over a decade, I have designed, maintained, and grown one of the world's largest hip resurfacing outcome registries - building not only the databases that preserve clinical evidence, but also the analytical workflows, publications, and scientific communication that allow that evidence to influence patient care and practice policy decisions. I work in the uncommon space where data architecture, statistical analysis, and scientific storytelling overlap. I build systems that make research possible, then help translate those findings into publications, conference presentations, regulatory submissions, and conversations clinicians can actually use.

Why “The Quill & the Query”?

Research isn’t finished when the data are collected.

A registry is only valuable if its evidence can be trusted, understood, and shared.

The Query represents careful investigation: designing databases, writing SQL, validating analyses, and asking questions worthy of the data.

The Quill represents translation: turning statistics into manuscripts, presentations, visualizations, and conversations that make complex evidence accessible.

Philosophy

I believe good research begins long before statistical analysis.

It begins with thoughtful data collection, careful stewardship, reproducible systems, and a willingness to ask questions that the original study was never designed to answer.

My favorite projects are those measured not in months, but in decades.

Because well-kept evidence compounds.

A registry maintained today may answer questions no one has thought to ask yet.
