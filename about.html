About the Scholar

One person, two crafts: the query that finds the data, and the quill that communicates it.

Most research departments divide the labor in a team that gathers the data, the people who analyze it, and the people who write it and communicate it. They rarely all share the same office, let alone the same desk. But that is the case for me. For nearly two decades I have been the architect of a longitudinal orthopedic outcomes registry, the analyst who queries it, and the writer who transforms these findings into the peer-reviewed literature and carries its findings into the world of research communication.

I am an architect of research systems. SQL, careful data dictionaries, and automated workflows turn retrospective chaos into answerable questions. It is quiet work, and it is the reason the loud work, the survivorship curves and the published papers, can be trusted.

The Quill & the Query is the working name for that whole practice: evidence, carefully kept and clearly told.
